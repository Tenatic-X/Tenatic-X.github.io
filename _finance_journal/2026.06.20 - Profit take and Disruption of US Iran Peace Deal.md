## **Lesson**:

I've learnt to try identify the expansiveness of the problem. From `small` (samsung strike, aka company-side), `medium` (broadcom disappointment, aka sector-side), to `large` (soh restriction, aka geopolitical-side). The next thing is to learn about persistence of these problems. I realized that fed rates, even though is classified as `large`, is of short persistence, mostly because it's a singular number with pretty much a single announcement/news event. Unlike the SOH, where its long persistence comes from the ever evolving geopolitical tension, wars, bomb strikes, failed negotiations, etc.

So in terms of identifying the right type of `market breaker`, they would be sorted into a 2d 3 by 3 grid. First axis is how many different sectors and markets would the `breaker` affect, and the second axis is how long that `breaker` will stay effective.
