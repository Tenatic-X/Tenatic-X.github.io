## **Lesson**:

Like bear markets, this volatile choppy market right seems best to be sat out, as I would likely lose all my returns because my stock bets are only availably placed within specific windows of time, making getting out even more difficult.

In addition, VIX of <20 says is best for well performing markets. But I realize like most other data tracking of whether a recession is occuring or not, is really deterministic of how much the values have changed from present day, to how many days in the past. It's not exactly the value that kills your gains, its the amount the value has changed.

High VIX is a classic sign of volatility to be aware of and hold back your capital, but during low VIX, it's also important to see how much percent VIX has moved and in what direction - the current regime is extremely choppy and not kind to high risk stocks.
