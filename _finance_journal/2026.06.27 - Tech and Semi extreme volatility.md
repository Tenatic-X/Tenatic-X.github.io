## **Lesson**:

Just because the Semi and Tech Storage stocks are heavily outperforming everyone despite everyone else lagging, does not mean it'll stay that way - the most important factor to this is their customers that directly buy their product. Apple, Microsoft, Open AI, etc. Just because your product is bottlenecked and in high demand, doesn't mean it'll remain that way if the customers whom buy it, don't see the returns from the product they bought from you.

The stock ecosysytem operates like a big sewage pipeline - where the one at the start of the chain is often most stable but hits less gain such as ASML and TSMC, while the one at the end of the chain absorbs all the risks like MSFT and TSLA, but gets all the gains. If the pipe at the end fails, the rest of the system will slowly breakdown as well until a certain point as the more you work your way down, the more stable it gets.

We're now at the point of the regime where mag 7s have been choppy and slowly bleeding out, and they're often the ones at the end of the pipeline, directly offering services to their consumers. If the pipe is already starting to burst at the end, then it's only a matter of time before it'll ripple back towards Micron, Sandisk, and KOSPI.

However when Tech bleeds out over `hypothetical gains` and overvalue, rather than anything financial like the 2008 crisis, people often reposition their money to sectors that have set and very visible earnings and returns. Because it's not like Healthcare or Consumerist products could disappear and not be needed, when physical care and goods to survive is essential regardless of market appetite.
