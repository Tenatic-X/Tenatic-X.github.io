## **Psych/Process**: 

The market has spoken, and it appears that the tech thesis is still holding well. Just that it most likely will not hold the thesis in a clean manner like it did 6 months ago. This is where the pivot from Healthcare stocks went back into bread and butter tech stocks - Any other hyper stocks like MU, SNDK, KOSPI, act more like commodities like gold and bitcoin, with opportunist investors infecting the stock for gains.

But also the tech sentiment during the opening week was only talked about being 'good' - no evident details really explained why tech grew on 1st and 2nd July. Knowing this may have been a good day to sell, I thought to wait it out a bit and see how the market plays. Having two back to back 'good' sentiment with no news holding the floor, sounds like market coping, the way it did when anticipating Micron's quarterly earnings.

Based on past information with major financial news, if my memory served me correctly, these are only temporary bruises that last for a day or two before reaching back to previous market value. Such as the example of the federal rate anouncement, where the dip in the market didn't even last a half the trading day before positive oil news brought everything back up.

It also helped that during Thursday early market closure, KOSPI performed well on their open during Friday and made gains despite the Job numbers from the previous day.

So far there hasn't seem to be tech changing news that fundamentally flip the thesis around yet, as the ones that do change are minor, or entirely based on market 'sentiment', with whimsical 180 changes constantly.

## **Lesson**:

Continued staying within the bread and butter tech stocks to capture the upside, with the least amount of volatility, and that to try capturing the 'sentiment' dips, and sell at 'sentiment' peaks if there are no news details that corroborate to the reasoning of market change.

Keeping an eye out of other sectors as well to check how the market are shifting their capital, because even though Healthcare had meteoric rise in the past few weeks, it actually has started to retreat again, likely that tech is reinstating its hold on the market.

## **Next plans**:

Continue the play with buying the dip, and then look to sell at the peak, as long as the information from the market corroborates to the overselling or overbuying sentiment.
