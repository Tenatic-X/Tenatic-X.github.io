### Proofreading AI code

The biggest problem I have is impatience for the code portion, and wanting to reach the results as fast as possible - because of the failed attempts that occured from Nasdaq, prrofreading the code allowed me to better understand how everything went under the hood with the help of chatbots explaining it to me, but also finesse out the bugs and actual problems with the code, which allowed me to retest and try again.

### Neverending code lookahead bias
Because of the nature of the task I'm working towards which is timebased, yet utilizing AI generated code, there is bound to be places where the code leaks future data, and ruins the whole 'returns' values for stock trading on past data.

So it's always a never ending cat and mouse chase - especially when I as a cat, am too lazy to hunt and track the mice as well lol. Hope to be better at being more proactive with code proofreading, as it eats up days of work and effort due to pure laziness and mental fatigue.

### Complicated exit strategies don't always work
You might think that adding specific rules for either exits, holds, delayed tracking, changing rules when threshold is reached etc. etc. could help with retaining the strong stock growths, whilst dropping unprofitable ones - unfortunately sometimes simple is best.

As adding more convoluted and complex strategies can just as easily pull in the bad tickers in with the good tickers, leading to often worse performance. Here for example is one where a signal is triggered, we track the entry exit logic strategy in the stock, but delay our entry until a certain number of days is reached.

### Quick to enter, quick to exit
Because we are strategy testing on Bear markets, what the backtesting strategy has found out, is that once a signal is triggered, the stock must be bought immediately. Then if at any point the stock drops between open to close, or close to open, the stock must be exited.

As currently, the backtester is unable to determine what stocks will continue dropping, or whichever is dropping a little before rallying - so the best option that is discovered, is treat every dip as if it is a full on pullback.
